What is EDUBA?

Born from a passion for learning and inspired by the ancient schools of Babylon, EDUBA exists to champion educators in a rapidly changing world. Our ethos is simple: we focus on real classroom challenges and build solutions from the ground up—letting teachers, academic researchers, and technologists co-create the best possible environment for students.

Drawing on experience that spans cryptography, avionics, brand strategy, and hands-on classroom leadership, our team knows that the disruption caused by technology can be turned into an opportunity. By partnering with educators, we explore and refine new ways of teaching—staying agile, innovative, and always collaborative.

Our Mission & Approach

EDUBA’s core mission is to empower educators to shape technology rather than be shaped by it. We offer:

  • Hands-On Collaboration: Workshops and “Labs” where educators guide the direction of new tools, ensuring any tech solution is tuned to real needs.
  • Research-Backed Insight: Collaborations with academic labs—like ongoing work with a Neuropolitics postdoc—that help us understand how technology impacts both teaching and learning.
  • Adaptive Development: We’re constantly iterating, testing, and listening to feedback. Educators act as product consultants, helping us build interfaces that feel natural and useful.

Whether it’s refining how next-generation interfaces integrate into lesson planning or developing platforms that let teachers explore and experiment, we believe in creating technology that genuinely elevates the classroom experience.

Reaching Educators

We understand educators have real concerns: time constraints, overloaded ed-tech stacks, and a genuine worry about where technology fits in a student’s life. Our solution is to build from their perspective:

  • 1:1 Interviews & Group Sessions: We host face-to-face and virtual “Group Therapy” discussions where teachers share pain points and discover new ways to integrate tech into their workflows.
  • Continuous Feedback Loops: Through cohort WhatsApp groups and online surveys, educators become active “Product Development Consultants,” helping shape each iteration of our solutions.
  • Live Demos & Events: Held at dedicated “Futures Institutes,” these events allow educators to network, test prototypes, and co-create improvements.

By centering education in every conversation, we help teachers see not just what’s possible with emerging technology, but how to make it their own.
